MMUKO-OS Canonical Boot Specification
Version: 0.2.0
Status: canonical
Source of truth: this document

[Boot Phases]
1 | PHASE_NEED_STATE_INIT | N | Need-state initialization | Validate Tier 1 physiological inputs and seed the boot handoff with HOLD defaults.
2 | PHASE_SAFETY_SCAN | S | Safety scan | Validate Tier 2 safety inputs and the minimum firmware safety envelope.
3 | PHASE_IDENTITY_CALIBRATION | I | Identity calibration | Bind operator identity, temporal frame, and calibration context into the handoff record.
4 | PHASE_GOVERNANCE_CHECK | G | Governance check | Confirm policy, provenance, and execution-authority constraints before loading the filesystem target.
5 | PHASE_INTERNAL_PROBE | I | Internal probe | Probe NSIGII firmware health, memory map integrity, and runtime compatibility signals.
6 | PHASE_INTEGRITY_VERIFICATION | I | Integrity verification | Verify discriminant, artifact manifest, and kernel entry constraints before handoff.

contract_rule = The bootloader must fully populate MMUKO_BOOT_HANDOFF before attempting kernel transfer.

[NSIGII Firmware Requirements]
fw_1 = Implement all six canonical phases using the exact phase identifiers listed above.
fw_2 = Default the handoff outcome to HOLD until integrity verification promotes it to PASS.
fw_3 = Abort with ALERT when Tier 1 or Tier 2 requirements resolve to NO.
fw_4 = Persist the filesystem target, kernel artifact path, and manifest path in the handoff contract.
fw_5 = Expose phase completion, outcome codes, and validation flags to native runtime consumers.
fw_6 = Reject handoff if the kernel artifact or manifest cannot be resolved from the FAT12 target.
fw_7 = Treat NSIGII firmware compatibility as a precondition for the kernel handoff contract.
